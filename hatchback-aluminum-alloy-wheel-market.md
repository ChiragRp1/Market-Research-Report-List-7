<p><p>The hatchback aluminum alloy wheel market is primarily segmented into casting and forging types. Casting involves pouring molten aluminum into molds to create intricate wheel designs, offering flexibility in shapes and sizes at lower production costs. In contrast, forging utilizes high-pressure techniques to reshape aluminum into a stronger, more durable wheel, enhancing overall performance and weight reduction. Both methods cater to diverse consumer preferences, with casting favored for its affordability and design versatility, while forging appeals to those seeking enhanced strength and performance.</p></p>
