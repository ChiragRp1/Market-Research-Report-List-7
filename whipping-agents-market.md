<p><p>Whipping agents are additives used to create and stabilize foam in various products, primarily in the food and beverage industry. Nonionic whipping agents, such as certain proteins and surfactants, function without electrical charge, promoting stable foam without altering flavor. Ionic whipping agents, which include anionic and cationic types, utilize electrical charges to enhance foam structure but may impact taste or viscosity. Both types are essential for achieving desired textures and consistencies in whipped products like creams and mousses.</p></p>
