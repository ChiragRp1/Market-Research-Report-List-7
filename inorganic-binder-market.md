<p><p>The inorganic binder market comprises various types, each offering unique properties for different applications. Silicates, often used in construction, provide durability and moisture resistance. Phosphates enhance adhesion and fire resistance, making them suitable for specialized industrial applications. Oxides serve as stabilizers and can improve mechanical strength. Sulfates, typically found in cement, contribute to rapid setting and hardening. Borates are valued for their antifungal properties and can enhance the performance of adhesives and coatings. Each type plays a crucial role in various industries.</p></p>
