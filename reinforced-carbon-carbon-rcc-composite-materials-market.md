<p><p>Reinforced Carbon-Carbon (RCC) composite materials are advanced composites designed for high-temperature applications, particularly in aerospace and automotive sectors. The Liquid Impregnation Process involves saturating carbon fibers with a liquid resin to enhance mechanical properties and thermal stability. In contrast, the Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) method deposits carbon layers on reinforcing fibers through a vapor-phase reaction, providing exceptional strength and thermal resistance. Both processes contribute to the RCC market, optimizing performance in demanding environments.</p></p>
