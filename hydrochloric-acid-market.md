<p><p>The hydrochloric acid market is primarily divided into two types: synthetic hydrochloric acid and by-product hydrochloric acid. Synthetic hydrochloric acid is produced through dedicated industrial processes, often involving the reaction of sodium chloride with sulfuric acid, ensuring high purity and consistency for various applications. By-product hydrochloric acid, on the other hand, is generated as a secondary product during the production of other chemicals, often resulting in lower costs but varying purity levels, utilized primarily in industries like metal processing and food production.</p></p>
