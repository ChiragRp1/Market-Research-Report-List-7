<p><p>The fire-resistant flame-retardant cable market is primarily segmented into two types: halogen-containing cables and halogen-free cables. Halogen-containing cables release halogens when exposed to fire, which can produce toxic smoke and gases, making them less desirable in enclosed spaces. In contrast, halogen-free cables are designed to emit minimal smoke and toxic substances, enhancing safety during a fire. These cables are increasingly preferred in construction and automotive sectors due to stricter safety regulations and growing environmental awareness.</p></p>
