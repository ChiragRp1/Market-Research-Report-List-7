<p><p>Satellite imagery for precision farming includes two main types: optical imagery and radar imagery. Optical imagery captures visible light and enables farmers to assess crop health, monitor growth, and detect pests using detailed visual data. In contrast, radar imagery uses microwave signals to provide data on soil moisture and crop structure regardless of weather conditions or time of day. Both types are essential for informed decision-making, optimizing resource use, and enhancing overall agricultural productivity within precision farming practices.</p></p>
