<p><p>The bio-based polysaccharide thickener market is categorized into three main types: plant-based, seaweed-based, and others. Plant-based thickeners, derived from sources like starches and gums, are widely used in food and cosmetic applications for their natural properties. Seaweed-based thickeners, such as carrageenan and agar, are sourced from marine algae, offering unique gelling and stabilizing abilities. The "others" category includes thickeners from fungi or bacteria, which are utilized in specialized applications across various industries, including food, pharmaceuticals, and personal care.</p></p>
