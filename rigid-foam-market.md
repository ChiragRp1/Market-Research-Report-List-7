<p><p>The rigid foam market consists of several types, each with unique properties and applications. Polyurethane (PU) foam is known for its excellent insulation and durability, making it popular in construction and refrigeration. Polystyrene (PS) foam is lightweight and often used in packaging and insulation. Polyethylene (PE) foam is versatile and resistant to moisture, suitable for cushioning and insulation. Polypropylene (PP) foam offers good chemical resistance and strength, often employed in automotive applications. Polyvinyl Chloride (PVC) foam is valued for its rigidity and weather resistance, used in building materials. Other types may include various specialty foams for niche applications.</p></p>
