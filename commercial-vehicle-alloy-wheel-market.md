<p><p>The commercial vehicle alloy wheel market comprises three main types: casting, forging, and others. Casting involves pouring molten metal into molds to create wheels, offering intricate designs and cost-effectiveness. Forging, on the other hand, utilizes high pressure to shape metal, resulting in stronger, lighter wheels preferred for heavy-duty applications. The "Other" category encompasses alternative manufacturing methods, such as extrusion and machining, catering to niche markets or specific vehicle requirements. Each type serves distinct performance and aesthetic needs in commercial vehicles.</p></p>
