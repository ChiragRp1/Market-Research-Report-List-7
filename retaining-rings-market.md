<p><p>The retaining rings market is segmented into external and internal retaining rings. External retaining rings are designed to fit into a groove on the outside of a shaft, securing components in place. They are commonly used in applications like gears and bearings. Internal retaining rings, on the other hand, fit into a groove on the inside of a bore, holding parts such as pins and sleeves. Both types play crucial roles in assemblies across various industries, ensuring stability and functionality.</p></p>
