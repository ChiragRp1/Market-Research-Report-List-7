<p><p>The organic caramel color market is segmented into four classes based on processing methods and applications. Class I is made from sugar solutions without sulfites, providing a mild flavor and light color, typically used in non-alcoholic beverages. Class II involves sulfites and has a darker hue, suited for baked goods. Class III, with a more pronounced flavor, is commonly employed in sauces and dressings. Class IV is the darkest and most intense, used in products requiring strong color, like dark sodas and candies.</p></p>
