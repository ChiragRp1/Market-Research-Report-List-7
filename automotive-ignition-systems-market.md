<p><p>The automotive ignition systems market is segmented by engine type: diesel and gasoline. Diesel engines use compression ignition, where air is compressed to a high temperature, igniting the fuel without a spark plug. Gasoline engines rely on spark ignition systems, using spark plugs to ignite the air-fuel mixture. Diesel systems are robust and fuel-efficient, suited for heavy-duty applications, while gasoline systems are common in passenger vehicles, offering smoother performance and lower emissions. Both systems are integral to engine efficiency and performance.</p></p>
