<p><p>Solid-state batteries for electric vehicles (EVs) are categorized into two main types: polymer-based solid-state batteries and those utilizing inorganic solid electrolytes. Polymer-based solid-state batteries employ flexible polymer electrolytes, offering advantages like lightweight construction and improved safety. In contrast, batteries with inorganic solid electrolytes utilize ceramic materials, providing higher energy densities and better thermal stability. Both types aim to enhance EV performance by increasing range, reducing charging times, and improving safety compared to traditional lithium-ion batteries.</p></p>
