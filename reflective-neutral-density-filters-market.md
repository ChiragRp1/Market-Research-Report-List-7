<p><p>Reflective Neutral Density Filters are categorized based on optical density and shape. Optical density classifications include less than 2, 2-4, and more than 4, determining the degree of light attenuation these filters provide. The less than 2 range offers subtle light reduction, while the 2-4 range allows for more significant dampening, and over 4 is suitable for extreme light control applications. In terms of shape, round filters are commonly used for lenses, while rectangular filters offer versatility for various mounting systems and broader applications.</p></p>
